**Full version (~60 sec — for more technical interviewers):**
> "My research is in photodynamic therapy for cutaneous malignancies at Cleveland Clinic. The core question we've been investigating is whether you can turn PDT — which is already a localized treatment — into something more immunogenic by combining it with pro-differentiating agents. We've used Vitamin D and 5-fluorouracil as combination partners and shown, in murine models of actinic keratosis, squamous cell carcinoma, and melanoma, that these combinations enhance both innate and adaptive immune responses. Mechanistically, we've looked at intratumoral immune cell recruitment, transcriptomic signatures, and signaling pathway activation using flow cytometry and RNAseq. I've also been involved in developing a singlet oxygen luminescence sensor for real-time dosimetry during PDT.
