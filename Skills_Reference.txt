1. HOT PAPER READER
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Searches PubMed + bioRxiv for the single most relevant, high-impact recent
  paper at the intersection of 3 keywords you provide. Writes a full expert
  summary to a PDF on your Desktop.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  Just type this in Claude Code:

    "Topic for today: <keyword1>, <keyword2>, and <keyword3>"

  Examples:
    "Topic for today: SGLT2 inhibitors, heart failure, and outcomes"
    "Topic for today: GLP-1 agonists, obesity, and cardiovascular risk"
    "Topic for today: CRISPR, gene editing, and cancer"

WHAT YOU GET
  PDF saved to Desktop:
    ~/Desktop/HotPaper_<Keyword1>_<Keyword2>_<Keyword3>_<Date>.pdf

  PDF contains:
    - Full paper citation
    - Why the paper matters
    - Background
    - Key methods
    - Main findings (with numbers)
    - Conclusions & implications
    - 5 presenter talking points

GOOD TO KNOW
  - Prioritizes papers with citations >= 20 or journal IF > 10
  - If nothing meets the bar, it tells you and picks the closest match
  - Does not fabricate citation counts or impact factors
  - After saving the PDF, Claude asks: "Save to Zotero? [y/N]"
    Say y to add the paper (title, authors, journal, DOI, PMID) to your Zotero library

2. SPECIFIC PAPER READER
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Takes a paper you already know you want to read and generates a clinical
  expert summary — framed for a healthcare professional. Includes level of
  evidence, critical appraisal, NNT/NNH, guideline alignment, and 3–5
  related papers to read next.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  Provide one of the following:

    - A PubMed ID:  "Summarize this paper: PMID 38291045"
    - A URL:        "Review this paper: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/..."
    - A local PDF:  "Summarize this paper: /path/to/article.pdf"
    - Pasted text:  Just paste the abstract or full text and ask for a summary

WHAT YOU GET
  PDF saved to Desktop:
    ~/Desktop/PaperReview_<AuthorLastName>_<Year>_<Date>.pdf

  PDF contains:
    - Study design + level of evidence banner
    - Clinical framing (2-3 sentence relevance summary)
    - Full paper header (title, authors, journal, DOI, PMID, citations, funding)
    - Clinical relevance
    - Background & clinical context
    - Study design & methods
    - Key findings (with effect sizes, CIs, p-values, NNT/NNH)
    - Critical appraisal (strengths, limitations, risk of bias)
    - Conclusions & practice implications
    - 5 clinical talking points
    - 3–5 related papers with relevance notes

ZOTERO INTEGRATION (OPT-IN)
  After saving the PDF, Claude asks: "Save to Zotero? [y/N]"
  Say y to add the paper (title, authors, journal, DOI, PMID) to your Zotero library.
  Credentials stored at: ~/Desktop/Claude Code/Claude_For_School/config/zotero_config.json

DIFFERENCE FROM HOT PAPER READER
  Hot Paper Reader = you give keywords, it FINDS the paper for you
  Specific Paper Reader = you already have a paper, it does a deep read

3. MEDARTICLE TO NOTEBOOKLM
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Takes research articles, UpToDate exports, or review articles and converts
  them into a clean, structured .txt file for NotebookLM. Auto-detects whether
  to use PICO schema (research articles) or Clinical schema (reviews/UpToDate).

  NOTE: For lecture slides (PPTX or PDF), use skill #4 lecture-to-notebooklm instead.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  "prepare for notebooklm: <source>"
  "notebooklm: <source>"
  "structure this for notebooklm: <source>"
  "ingest this for notebooklm: <source>"

  Source can be any of:
    - A PubMed ID:    "prepare for notebooklm: PMID 38291045"
    - A URL:          "notebooklm: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/..."
    - A local PDF:    "prepare for notebooklm: /path/to/article.pdf"
    - Pasted text:    Paste any UpToDate export, review article, etc.

  You can also specify a schema manually if needed:
    "prepare for notebooklm using PICO schema: ..."
    "prepare for notebooklm using clinical schema: ..."

AUTO-DETECTED SCHEMAS

  PICO (used for research articles / RCTs / meta-analyses):
    Source Info → Why This Paper Matters → Population → Intervention →
    Comparison → Outcomes → Key Findings → Limitations →
    Clinical Takeaway → Key Concepts for NotebookLM

  Clinical (used for lectures, UpToDate, review articles):
    Source Info → Overview → Pathophysiology → Clinical Presentation →
    Diagnosis → Management → Complications →
    Key Concepts for NotebookLM

WHAT YOU GET
  .txt file saved to:
    ~/Desktop/Claude Code/Claude_For_School/NotebookLM/

  Naming:
    Research article:  NLM_PICO_<AuthorLastName>_<Year>_<Date>.txt
    Clinical content:  NLM_Clinical_<topic_slug>_<Date>.txt

HOW TO UPLOAD TO NOTEBOOKLM
  NotebookLM → your notebook → Sources → Upload → select the .txt file

WHY .TXT AND NOT .MD
  NotebookLM accepts .txt natively. Markdown (.md) is not a supported format.
  The structured sections in the .txt file are written so NotebookLM's AI
  can immediately produce useful summaries and answer targeted questions.

4. LECTURE TO NOTEBOOKLM
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Takes raw lecture slides (PPTX or PDF) and converts them into a structured
  .md file with bolded high-yield terms, ready for NotebookLM upload. Applies
  a schema matched to your training year — Step 1 or Step 2.

  STEP 1 SCHEMA:  Normal Physiology → Pathophysiology → Pharmacology →
                  Clinical Correlation → High-Yield Points
  STEP 2 SCHEMA:  Overview → Pathophysiology → Clinical Presentation →
                  Workup/Diagnosis → Management → Complications →
                  High-Yield Points

  Both schemas end with Key Concepts for NotebookLM.
  High-yield terms are **bolded** throughout the document.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  "lecture: /path/to/slides.pptx"
  "slides: /path/to/CardiacPhysiology.pptx"
  "process lecture: /path/to/HeartFailure.pptx"
  "prep slides: /path/to/file.pptx"

  Also works with lecture PDFs or pasted lecture transcripts.

SCHEMA AUTO-DETECTION
  Claude auto-detects Step 1 vs Step 2 from the topic/course name:
    Step 1 → basic science courses (physiology, pharmacology, biochemistry,
             microbiology, immunology, anatomy, embryology, histology)
    Step 2 → named diseases and clinical management lectures

  For ambiguous topics (e.g., "Hypertension") Claude will confirm in one
  line before proceeding: "This looks like a Step 1 lecture — correct?"

WHAT YOU GET
  .md file saved to:
    ~/Desktop/Claude Code/Claude_For_School/NotebookLM/

  Naming:
    Step 1 lecture:  NLM_Lec_Step1_<topic_slug>_<Date>.md
    Step 2 lecture:  NLM_Lec_Step2_<topic_slug>_<Date>.md

HOW TO UPLOAD TO NOTEBOOKLM
  NotebookLM → your notebook → Sources → Upload → select the .md file

WHY .MD AND NOT .TXT
  The lecture skill outputs .md (not .txt) so that **bolded high-yield terms**
  render correctly. NotebookLM accepts .md files. If you ever need to switch
  formats, just ask Claude to convert it.

HANDLING IMAGE-ONLY SLIDES
  PPTX slides that contain only images (no text) are flagged in the output:
    [Slide N: image-only — <slide title>. Review slide manually.]
  You won't miss diagram-heavy slides — you'll just need to review them yourself.

DEPENDENCIES
  python-pptx  REQUIRED for PPTX input
               Install: pip3 install python-pptx
               (Claude will auto-install if missing when you run the skill)

5. NOTEBOOKLM TO ANKI
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Reads any NotebookLM output, generates comprehensive Anki cards with
  context-detected card types (Cloze for mechanisms/pathways, Basic Q&A for
  definitions/criteria/drug pairings), applies double-tagging, and delivers
  cards via Anki-Connect (if Anki is open) or a .apkg file (fallback).

ANKI-CONNECT DIRECT IMPORT (DEFAULT)
  If Anki is open and the AnkiConnect add-on is running (localhost:8765), cards
  are injected directly into Anki — no file import needed. If Anki is closed,
  falls back to .apkg generation (deck name: MedSchool).

DECK ORGANIZATION (canonical — applies forever)
  ALL med school cards go into ONE home deck named exactly "MedSchool".
  Never create per-lecture or per-date subdecks.
  Never touch "Undergrad MCAT (Austin)" — that is a separate pre-med deck.
  Organization is by TAGS, not by decks.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  "make anki cards from: <source>"
  "anki: <source>"
  "notebooklm to anki: <source>"
  "generate cards from: <source>"

  Source can be:
    - A .txt file:    "make anki cards from: /Desktop/Claude Code/Claude_For_School/NotebookLM/NLM_PICO_Smith_2024_2026-03-05.txt"
    - A .pdf file:    "anki: /path/to/lecture_notes.pdf"
    - A .mp3 file:    "anki: /path/to/notebooklm_audio.mp3"  (requires Whisper — see below)
    - Pasted text:    Paste a NotebookLM study guide and ask for cards

CARD TYPE LOGIC (auto-detected per fact)

  CLOZE cards are generated for:
    - Mechanisms ("X leads to Y via Z")
    - Physiological pathways or cascades
    - Drug mechanisms of action
    - Sequences and ordered steps

  BASIC Q&A cards are generated for:
    - Definitions
    - Named criteria / scoring systems (CURB-65, Wells, CHADS-VASc, etc.)
    - Drug name ↔ indication / drug class pairings
    - Lab values and thresholds
    - Classic clinical presentations
    - First-line management
    - Complications

  High-yield facts get BOTH a cloze AND a basic card.

7-AXIS TAG SCHEMA (every card gets all applicable axes)
  Course::<code>        e.g. Course::OST510  (course/block)
  System::<system>      e.g. System::Cardiovascular  (body system or discipline)
  Source::<material>    e.g. Source::FACts, Source::Lecture, Source::Pathoma
  Yield::High|Mid|Low   e.g. Yield::High
  Boards::<section>     e.g. Boards::Cardiology  (First Aid / COMLEX section)
  Exam::<id>            e.g. Exam::2026-07-16  (optional — only when exam date known)
  Doc::<document_name>  e.g. Doc::Basic_Anatomical_Terminology  (AUTO — derived from source filename)
                        Enables per-document filtered decks via the filtered-deck skill.
                        Basename of source file, no extension, spaces/hyphens → underscores.
                        For pasted text with no filename, Claude asks for a short name or
                        falls back to the detected topic in underscore form (e.g. Doc::Heart_Failure).

  Example: ["Course::OST510","System::Anatomy","Source::FACts","Yield::High","Boards::Anatomy","Exam::2026-07-16","Doc::Basic_Anatomical_Terminology"]

WHAT YOU GET
  .apkg file saved to (Path B fallback only):
    ~/Desktop/Claude Code/Claude_For_School/Anki/

  Naming:   Anki_<TopicSlug>_<Date>.apkg
  Deck name in Anki: MedSchool

HOW TO IMPORT INTO ANKI (Path B fallback only)
  Anki → File → Import → select the .apkg file
  It merges into the MedSchool deck automatically.

CARD VOLUME TARGETS
  Short source (<500 words):      15–25 cards
  Medium source (500–1500 words): 30–50 cards
  Long source (>1500 words):      50–80 cards

DEPENDENCIES
  genanki:         INSTALLED (pip3 install genanki)
  openai-whisper:  NOT installed — only needed for .mp3 input
                   Install with: pip3 install openai-whisper

THE INTENDED WORKFLOW
  This is your glue layer between NotebookLM and Anki:

    Lecture slides (.pptx)          Research article / UpToDate
          ↓  [lecture-to-notebooklm]      ↓  [medarticle-to-notebooklm]
    .md in NotebookLM folder        .txt in NotebookLM folder
                        ↓  (upload to NotebookLM → study, ask questions, synthesize)
                  NotebookLM study guide / audio overview
                        ↓  [notebooklm-to-anki]
                  .apkg imported under Claude deck in Anki
                        ↓  (daily review alongside AnKing)
                  Retention

6. CLINICAL VIGNETTE COACH
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  A Socratic clinical reasoning tutor. You submit a vignette + your full
  reasoning chain (H&P → DDx → Workup → Management), and Claude guides you
  toward your gaps through targeted questions before revealing a structured
  gap analysis. Sessions are logged so you can track recurring blind spots.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  "coach me on this vignette: ..."
  "clinical coach: ..."
  "let's do a case: ..."
  "vignette: ..."
  "practice case: ..."

VIGNETTE SOURCES (all supported)
  - Paste from a question bank (UWorld, Amboss, shelf review, etc.)
  - Write your own based on something you're studying
  - Ask Claude to generate one: "generate a case on [topic]"

WHAT HAPPENS EACH SESSION

  Phase 0 — Setup (Claude always asks these — never assumed):
    1. Exam context: STEP 1/2/3 or COMLEX Level 1/2/3?
    2. Source document? (optional — provide a .txt from your NotebookLM folder
       for Claude to check your reasoning against)

  Phase 1 — You submit your reasoning:
    Write out freely:
      H&P: What key findings stand out?
      DDx: Top 3 diagnoses and why?
      Workup: What do you order and in what order?
      Management: What's the plan if your leading Dx is confirmed?

  Phase 2 — Socratic dialogue (2-4 rounds):
    Claude asks probing questions to guide you toward gaps.
    Does NOT hand out the answer immediately.
    Will give hints if you're close but not quite there.
    Type "just tell me" to skip to the gap analysis.

  Phase 3 — Gap analysis reveal:
    Framework adherence → H&P interpretation (hits + misses) →
    DDx quality → Workup → Management →
    Key learning points → Source contradictions flagged

EXAM-SPECIFIC FOCUS
  STEP 1 / COMLEX L1  — Pathophysiology, mechanisms, basic science connections
  STEP 2 / COMLEX L2  — Next best step, clinical decisions, management algorithms
  STEP 3 / COMLEX L3  — Monitoring, prognosis, long-term management, complications
  COMLEX (any)        — OMM considerations added if you request it

CONTRADICTION HANDLING (strict — no hallucination)
  If your source document conflicts with Claude's knowledge:
    - Claude stops immediately and flags it before proceeding
    - You decide: use source doc / use standard knowledge / note both
    - All contradictions are logged in the session file

WHAT YOU GET
  Chat-based gap analysis (in-session)
  Session log saved to:
    ~/Desktop/Claude Code/Claude_For_School/CaseReview/
  Naming: CaseReview_<TopicSlug>_<Date>.md

  Log contains: vignette, your reasoning, full Socratic exchange,
  gap analysis, key learning points, contradictions, pattern notes field.

  On future sessions, Claude will check if you've done a similar case
  before and vary the Socratic angle accordingly.

POST-SESSION ACTIONS (NEW — Phase 6)
  After the gap analysis, Claude will ask:
  "(A) add learning points to Anki, (B) schedule a calendar review, (C) both, (D) skip"

  Option A — Anki: adds the 3 key learning points as Basic cards to Claude::CaseReview
              (requires Anki open with AnkiConnect)
  Option B — Calendar: creates an Apple Calendar event with a 15-min reminder
              (requires apple-calendar MCP — will prompt for access on first use)

NO DEPENDENCIES REQUIRED
  Fully chat-based. No Python scripts or external libraries needed.

7. SYLLABUS TO CALENDAR
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Parses a course syllabus (PDF, image, or pasted text) and bulk-creates
  Google Calendar events for every lecture, lab, exam, deadline, and
  orientation item found. Shows a preview table before creating anything.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  Type any of these in Claude Code:

    /syllabus
    syllabus: /path/to/syllabus.pdf
    "parse my syllabus"
    "import syllabus to calendar"

  Accepts:
    - PDF file path (Claude reads it directly)
    - Image/screenshot path (Claude sees it)
    - Pasted syllabus text

WORKFLOW
  1. Provide syllabus (file path or paste)
  2. Claude extracts all events with date/time/title/type
  3. Preview table shown — confirm or filter (e.g. "exams only")
  4. Events created on your Google Calendar in bulk
  5. Summary reported (X/Y events created)

FILTER OPTIONS (say after preview)
  "yes"                  — create all events
  "exams only"           — create only exam-type events
  "skip lectures"        — create everything except lectures
  "just exams and deadlines" — create filtered subset

NOTES
  - Recurring weekly lectures are expanded into individual events (not recurrences)
    so holiday exceptions are handled correctly
  - All-day events are used for deadlines with no time given
  - Default exam duration is 2 hours if end time is not given
  - Requires Google Calendar MCP to be active (gcal_create_event)

DESIGN DOC (Anki integration)
  Claude_For_School/anki-integration-plan.md — algorithm for integrating
  the MSUCOM upperclassman Anki deck when it arrives (unsuspend by block/
  lecture, coverage tracking, bot commands). Build pending deck arrival.

8. COURSE PREP  (orchestrator)
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Turns a whole folder of course materials into a complete study pack. Point it
  at a course folder that has (a) an objectives / "FACts" / learning-goals doc and
  (b) source materials (pptx/docx/pdf). It produces:
    - an objective-by-objective study guide (.md, saved to _outputs/)
    - an Anki deck (.apkg, saved to the Anki/ folder)
    - spaced reviews + the quiz/exam date on the Medical School calendar
    - a live active-recall quiz on the objectives

CANONICAL FOLDER LAYOUT (added 2026-06-25 — keeps the automation from re-processing
or eating its own output as the semester grows). Each course folder is organized:
    <Course>/
      _inbox/      <- YOU drop new slides/PDFs here. The skill reads ONLY this.
      _processed/  <- skill moves files here after carding them = the "done" record
      _outputs/    <- study guides + .apkg copies (never re-read as a source)
      objectives/  <- the FACts / objectives / learning-goals docs
  Your only habit all semester: drop new files in _inbox/. Each run processes only
  what's there, then moves them to _processed/, so nothing is ever re-chewed and the
  generated study guide can't be mistaken for a source. To see what's already done,
  look in _processed/ or check the Doc:: tags in Anki (one per carded document).

HOW TO TRIGGER
  "course prep: <folder path>"      "prep this folder"      "FACts prep"
  "make a study pack from this folder"   or just drop a course folder path
  "get me ready for the <X> quiz" (with a materials folder)

EXECUTION MODEL (Opus brain, Sonnet hand)
  Opus writes the coverage blueprint (every objective + term) and verifies the
  output; Sonnet does the bulk extraction + drafting against that blueprint;
  Opus runs the live quiz directly. This minimizes tokens AND missed details.

PIPELINE
  0. Intake — set up the _inbox/_processed/_outputs/objectives layout; read ONLY
            _inbox/ for new sources; find the objectives file + exam date
  1. Blueprint — Opus enumerates everything that must be covered
  2. Study guide — Sonnet drafts it from the sources (flags any (supp) fill-ins)
  3. Verify — Opus checks every blueprint item + spot-checks accuracy
  4. Anki — Opus blueprints cards, Sonnet builds the .apkg (notebooklm-to-anki rules);
            all cards go into the single "MedSchool" deck with the 7-axis tag schema
            (Course/System/Source/Yield/Boards/Doc/Exam); auto-imported via AnkiConnect;
            "Undergrad MCAT (Austin)" deck is never touched.
  4b. Mark processed — after guide + deck verify, move this run's sources from
            _inbox/ to _processed/ (prevents re-processing next run)
  5. Spaced reviews — propose dates, confirm, then write to the calendar
  6. Quiz — Opus quizzes interactively (answers withheld), routes weak topics to review

CHAINS THESE SKILLS
  lecture-to-notebooklm (deep slide structuring), notebooklm-to-anki (deck),
  clinical-vignette-coach (case-style quizzing), + Johnny's schedule_spaced_reviews.

NOTES
  - Confirms before any calendar write.
  - Grounds content in the provided materials; supplements are flagged, never faked.
  - Study guide goes to _outputs/ (not the folder root) so it is never re-read as
    a source on a later run.

9. FILTERED DECK BUILDER
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES
  Drills a precise slice of the single MedSchool home deck — one document,
  one system, one yield tier, or one exam — using an Anki filtered (dynamic)
  deck, without breaking the one-home-deck + tags architecture.

  Because AnkiConnect has no action to create filtered decks, the skill does
  the automatable parts and teaches the one 3-click manual step:
    1. Resolves the user's request to the correct Anki search string.
    2. Auto-applies a `Doc::<name>` tag via AnkiConnect addTags if missing.
    3. Gives the user the exact search string + click-by-click instructions
       to create the filtered deck (Tools -> Create Filtered Deck...) and
       rebuild it on demand with one click.

  The Doc:: tag axis (7th axis, added 2026-06-25) is the key tool here:
    Doc::<document_name>  — underscores for spaces, e.g. Doc::Basic_Anatomical_Terminology
  It lets you isolate every card from a single source document for targeted drilling.

HOW TO TRIGGER
  "filtered deck"
  "filter deck"
  "make a deck for just X"
  "drill only X"
  "study just X"
  "test me on <document/topic>"
  Any request to isolate a subset of MedSchool cards to review

TAG SCHEMA (7 axes — all used to target filtered decks)
  Course::<code>        e.g. Course::OST510
  System::<system>      e.g. System::Anatomy
  Source::<material>    e.g. Source::FACts
  Yield::High|Mid|Low
  Boards::<section>     e.g. Boards::Anatomy
  Exam::<id>            e.g. Exam::2026-07-16
  Doc::<document_name>  e.g. Doc::Basic_Anatomical_Terminology  ← NEW (2026-06-25)

FILTERED DECK CREATION (3-click manual — AnkiConnect limitation)
  In Anki: Tools -> Create Filtered Deck...
    Name:   Drill: <Topic>
    Search: tag:Doc::<name>   (or any search string)
    Build.  Done.
  Rebuild: click the filtered deck -> Rebuild to re-pull current matching cards.
  Empty:   returns all cards to MedSchool without studying.

NOTES
  - Cards return to MedSchool automatically after the filtered deck is emptied.
  - A card can only live in one filtered deck at a time; suspended cards excluded.
  - Never manually move cards between decks — always use tags + filtered decks.
